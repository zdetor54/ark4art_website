Vasilis Lekkas: The Greatest Greeks

On April 2, 1980, Vasilis Lekkas performs under the direction of Manos Hadjidakis, at the Municipal Theater of Piraeus, on live broadcast by the Third Program of the Hellenic Radio, and the journey from keynote to keynote begins... Through this journey into music he came together with and met the whole world. His Hellenic music needed no translation anywhere on the planet. "... You were a country that I made my homeland ..." He carries Apollo and Dionysus to the world, in a lyrical and revolutionary way, through his explosive interpretation. He was called "The Newest Zorba" by Mikis Theodorakis.

Always standing up with the aim of conveying the universal role of lyrics and Hellenic music, as he was taught through leading collaborations with composers and poets that shape the modern mythology of our country. All these, determine his choices and, very early, in the very beginning of his career, Vasilis Lekkas marks with his presence an artistic framework of a special ethos and style. For nearly forty years he travels the world with a repertoire that was inextricably linked to his name and highlights his relationship with an initiate audience which he feeds with stirring interpretations derived from within his pursuit to reveal the endless Hellenic music, with ultimate goal to pass it on to future generations.

Within the big music family he cooperates with Manos Hatzidakis, Mikis Theodorakis, Yannis Markopoulos, Giannis Spathas, Giorgos Trandalidis, Giorgos Kazantzis. Vasilis Lekkas is known for his theatrical presence that places him in the broader sense of an artist, with a unique mental and physical imprint. His amazing vocal abilities, along with his knowledge and technical training, allowed him to expand his interpretive boundaries, something that he reveals by singing charismatically in a varied musical landscape that includes: Hellenic contemporary, folk, country, as well as, the so called rebetiko song, the type of Hellenic urban folk music. His discography is an event that was generated, over the years, under the exploring prism of various sounds, rhythms and musical idioms, with the aim of highlighting a deeply social singing, consciously charged, uniting generations through its delivery.

Vasilis Lekkas is the Ambassador of the International Foundation for Greece.
